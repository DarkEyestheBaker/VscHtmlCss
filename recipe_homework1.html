This recipe makes 1 8"x8" pan.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 2 teaspoons instant espresso (or coffee) powder mixed with 1 tablespoon hot water
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
    • Do not skimp on the vanilla! ü§®
  • 2 eggs
    • You can put the eggs in a bowl with the vanilla. It's fine.
  • 1/2 cup flour
    • Surprisingly whole wheat flour is really good in these brownies! Who knew?
  • 1/3 cup cocoa powder - rounded
    • A little extra cocoa doesn't hurt. Trust me here.
    • Also, you can sub part or all of the cocoa with dark cocoa powder.
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips
    • Also, do not skimp on the chocolate chips!
    • You can add 1/2 cup chopped walnuts if you want. üòí But I will lose all respect for you. Just sayin'. ü§ê

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
  2. Grease or spray an 8-inch square pan. Set aside.
  3. Using a hand mixer or stand mixer, beat together melted butter, sugar, and coffee in a large mixing bowl.
      4. If you don't have a hand or stand mixer, you can use a whisk, a wooden spoon, or anything you've got really.
  4. While mixer is on, add eggs one at a time.
      5. Vanilla will be added if you included them with the eggs. If not, add after eggs and mix.
  5. Stir in flour, cocoa, baking powder, and salt just until blended.
      6. Don't over mix or your brownies will be tough. And not in a Ryan-Reynolds-action-movie way.
  6. Fold in chocolate chips.
  7. Pour into prepared pan and smooth out to edges and corners.
  8. Bake 20-25 minutes or until they pull away from the sides of the pan. Cool completely.
      9. If you can wait that long.
  9. Cut into squares. Makes 16.
      10. Or 4. It just depends on how you cut them and what your day's been like. ü§ï

Note: These are really great with ice cream, whipped cream, fruit, chocolate syrup, frosting, caramel topping, glass of milk, and/or pretty much anything or nothing. Enjoy!
